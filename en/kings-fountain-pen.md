1. Mr Grey is accused of

   * stealing a priceless object
   * stealing eighty wheels of cheese
   * working with a loan shark
   * entering the palace without a ticket

2. The prosecution points out that Mr Grey

   * was in debt, so he needed the money
   * is known for selling artefacts on the black market
   * has stolen many similar items before
   * is a dangerous and violent criminal

3. Mr Grey's lawyer says that Mr Grey

   * is a reformed criminal
   * had cleared his debt, so didn't need the money
   * is very sorry for stealing in the past
   * has a family who depend on him

4. She points out that

   * the thief's face is never seen in the security camera footage
   * dozens of people were seen entering the King's private room
   * the ticket found on the King's desk could be a fake
   * Mr Grey doesn't own a black jumper

5. It turns out that

   * the real thief was the judge himself
   * the judge wasn't a real judge: it was a disguise
   * Mr Grey *did* steal the pen
   * the King simply lost his pen
